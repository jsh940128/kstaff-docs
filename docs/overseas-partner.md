# Overseas Partner Overview

Welcome to K-Staff. As an **Overseas Partner**, you serve as the foundational supply link in our cross-border recruitment platform. You are responsible for sourcing, vetting, and managing the candidate pool that will be introduced to trusted employers in Korea.

K-Staff operates under a strict **privacy-first, operator-governed model**. This ensures maximum compliance, data security, and fairness for all parties involved.

---

## Your Role and Responsibilities

As an Overseas Partner, your primary operations exist entirely on the supply side of the platform. Your responsibilities include:
* **Talent Sourcing:** Gathering a high-quality pool of candidates seeking employment in Korea.
* **Data Accuracy:** Ensuring all candidate profiles, resumes, and supporting documentation are accurate and up-to-date.
* **Initial Screening:** Conducting the preliminary evaluation of candidates before submitting them to the K-Staff platform.

---

## How the K-Staff Privacy Firewall Works

To protect candidate data and ensure compliance with international regulations, K-Staff uses a tiered disclosure system. You provide the full candidate profile, but the platform strictly controls who sees what:

1. **Anonymized Browsing (Tier-1):** Employers initially only see non-identifying attributes (like target role, experience level, and job category). They cannot see names, contact information, or sensitive documents.
2. **Governed Access (Tier-2):** Only employers who have passed our strict vetting process and signed active compliance agreements can view structured resumes.
3. **Restricted Documents (Tier-3):** Highly sensitive PII and raw identity documents are strictly gated and managed by the platform.

## Platform Walkthrough

Managing your candidate pipeline is done entirely through the Overseas Portal. Here is a step-by-step guide to using the system.

### 1. The Dashboard
When you log in, you will see your main Dashboard. This provides a high-level overview of your candidate inventory, showing how many candidates are in Draft, Active, On Hold, or Placed statuses. From here, you can search for candidates or click **Create Candidate** to begin.

![Dashboard Overview](/img/overseas-1.png)

### 2. Creating a Candidate (Draft Mode)
When creating a new candidate, their status defaults to **Draft**. You will start by filling out the **Public Profile (Tier-1)**. This is the anonymized data that approved employers will use to filter and find talent.

![Register Candidate - Tier 1](/img/overseas-2.png)

### 3. Adding Resume & Experience Details
Navigate to the **Resume** tab to input the candidate's structured employment history and education. This data is critical for matching candidates to the right roles.

![Register Candidate - Resume](/img/overseas-4.png)

### 4. Uploading Verification Documents
In the **Documents** tab, you can upload necessary files such as PDF resumes or health certificates. You can use the "Mark as sensitive" toggle to ensure PII or identity documents are properly flagged for the privacy firewall.

![Register Candidate - Documents](/img/overseas-5.png)

### 5. Activating a Candidate (PII Requirements)
To make a candidate eligible for matching, you must change their status from Draft to **Active**. The system enforces strict data completeness; as shown in the red warning box, you cannot save an Active profile without filling out mandatory fields in both the Public Profile and the **Private Profile (PII)** tabs. 

*(Note: PII such as exact names, phone numbers, and passport details are hidden from employers by default).*

![Register Candidate - PII and Active Status](/img/overseas-3.png)

### 6. Managing Pipeline Status
Once a candidate is in the system, you can manage their availability directly from the dashboard by updating their Status (e.g., moving an Active candidate to **On Hold** if they are temporarily unavailable). You can also track their overall pipeline stage (like "Shortlisted by Platform").

![Changing Status](/img/overseas-6.png)

If you need to update any information, simply click **Edit** on the candidate's row to return to the edit screen and apply changes.

![Edit Candidate](/img/overseas-7.png)
