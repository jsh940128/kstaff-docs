# Korea Partner Overview

Welcome to K-Staff. As a **Korea Partner**, you are the vital execution link in our cross-border recruitment platform. While the Overseas Partners supply the talent and the Platform governs the compliance, you are responsible for local coordination, interview management, and final placement execution on the ground in Korea.

---

## Your Role and Access

Because you handle the sensitive logistics of interviewing and placing candidates, your organization is granted elevated access **only after** a demand request has been explicitly routed to you by the Platform.

* **Tier-2 & Tier-3 Access:** Once a demand list is routed to your organization and active Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) are in place, you gain access to the candidate's structured resume (Tier-2) and highly sensitive PII and verification documents (Tier-3) required to facilitate legal employment.

---

## Platform Walkthrough

Your primary interface is the Demand List management system. Here is a step-by-step guide to progressing candidates through the local pipeline.

### 1. Viewing Assigned Demand Lists
When an Employer submits a request and the Platform approves it, the demand list is routed directly to you. Your dashboard will show all demand lists currently assigned to your organization. Click **View Candidates** to proceed.

![Demand Lists Dashboard](/img/korea-1.png)

### 2. Accepting the Routing Assignment
Before you can interact with any candidates, you must officially accept the routing assignment from the Platform. A red banner will prompt you to **Accept Demand List**, signifying that you are taking responsibility for this pipeline.

![Accepting Demand List](/img/korea-2.png)

### 3. Managing the Candidate Pipeline
Once accepted, the Demand List moves to the `In Review` status. You now have full access to the candidate pipeline, separated by stages (Proposed, Locked, Interview, Placement, Placed, Terminal). From here, you can perform necessary actions to coordinate the hiring process.

![Candidate Pipeline](/img/korea-3.png)

### 4. Requesting an Interview
When you have coordinated with the Employer and are ready to schedule a formal interview, click the **Interview** button. You will submit preferred times and operational notes. The Platform Admin will review and approve this interview queue to ensure strict operational governance.

![Request Interview](/img/korea-5.png)

### 5. Locking Candidates for Processing
If the Employer decides to hire the candidate post-interview, you must execute a "Candidate Lock." Clicking the **Lock** button opens a fee click-wrap agreement. Agreeing to these service fee terms officially takes the candidate off the active market and secures exclusive processing responsibility for your organization.

![Lock Candidate](/img/korea-4.png)

### 6. Accessing Tier-3 Documents
To process local hiring logistics and legal requirements, you will need the candidate's verified documents. Clicking **Documents** opens the Candidate Profile, where you can safely download Tier-3 files (like PDF resumes or health certificates) and view your internal notes.

![Candidate Profile and Documents](/img/korea-candidate-profile.png)

### 7. Reporting Final Placement
Once the local hiring logistics and contracts are finalized, click the **Report** button to submit your placement evidence. You will provide the Contract Start Date and any necessary notes. This moves the candidate to the `placement_reported` status.

![Report Placement](/img/korea-6.png)

---

### Final Confirmation
After you submit the placement report, the K-Staff Platform Admin will conduct a final review, confirm the fee handshake, and transition the candidate to the terminal `placed` state, officially closing the loop on a successful cross-border hire.
